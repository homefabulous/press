What makes our platform different: As you’ve d already probably guessed, our competitors are services like; angieslist, homeadvisor, and others. Actually instead of  making matches, we provide many quotes to allow customers to find one that matches their budget and criteria, so that makes our nearest competitor thumbtack. Now, you may me wondering how is our platform different? well, thats easy…

We’ve done heavy research into this space before we ever started building anything, and we found two major flaws in thumbtacks platform that effects both customer and service provider in a negative way, and one more that effects the consumer. We’re simply fixing these issues with our own service.

Through asking existing users of thumbtack we discovered the first issue is the business model. The way it works is a service provider signs up, creates a profile, and awaits project request to bid on. In order to submit a bid the service provider then needs to purchase credit through thumbtack and use those credits to submit bids. This process sounds like a nifty business model, but it actually creates a few problems for both parties. Fist with this model the consumer really never gets what the service bills itself to provide, that is dozens of quotes. The credit system is the cause of this, and here’s why.A service provider is signed  up, they receive a project request to bid on, Being that they have to use credits to submit quotes, they will be hesitant to bid on small job, and would prefer to use the credit toward a larger jb with a bigger pay off. Now, since the majority of request will be small projects, this creates a problem. 

The problem being consumers fail to get the “dozens” of quotes they’re looking for. Also the service providers miss out on small projects that they normally would love to do, but they don’t want to waste the credits if they can bid on something bigger.

The solution: Although during our growth phase, the service will be 100% free, at some point we will need to monetize. Granted the credit systems seems good on paper, but in reality  it’s highly flawed as described above. To correct this we will charge a low flat monthly fee. This will allow our service providers to bid on all projects big or small with out hesitation. With this system, it will allow the service providers to bid more, and the consumers to get far more competitive quotes and options. 


2nd problem: This is by far the biggest flaw that thumbtack and other similar services have. This highly effects both consumer and service provider, and the solution is so insanely simple we still can’t wrap our heads around why it’s not being implemented by the major players in the space, but its just not being offered.

If you’re a consumer and you want to post you’re project on thumbtack, here’s how it goes…. You enter what you want done, you then provide a description, then contact info, that’s it. 

If your’re a service provider, you read the project description, you then use some credits to send a ball park estimate, then the consumer chooses the best fit and price. sounds simple right? Well it is simple, but highly flawed.

The flaw is… A service provider cannot possibly provide an accurate quote through a text description alone. Many times what happens is a service providers will submit a quote, say for $300, the consumer will think its right in their price range and accept. Once the service provider physically gets there and puts their eyeballs on it, they then have to tell th customer “look, I know I told you 300, but now that I see it, its going to be more like 3,000″ the price is now out of their price range and the whole concept becomes a complete waste of valuable time for both parties.

The solution: We only allow video submissions, that simple!. A picture is worth a thousand words, but a 3 minute video is worth 2 thousand pictures (actually at 10 frames per second on average, it’s 1800, but pretty close) With this system, the service provider gets a full view of the project, the customer can touch and fiddle with problem areas  this it gives the service providers a far greater understanding of what the project entails along with the material it will take to fix it. We know for a fact that the average person now is walking around with a video recorder in their pocket, so this concept is not far fetched at all.


The 3rd problem: The 3rd issue is where all competitors fail at miserably, this includes angieslist, homeadvisor, thumbtack, redbeacon, porch, and others. This problem is what they all bill themselves as being able to solve, but they never have really solved it. Allow me to explain.

All these companies promote themselves as finding the best pro for the job, but how are they doing this? Well, they’re not! See, all they’re really doing is developing systems to “try” and find the most trusted service providers at scale. I repeat “at scale” does this provide the best service? Hell no it doesn’t! The only thing this system does is benefit the best system manipulators. For example, if someone gets many great reviews from installing lighting fixtures, does this make them the best candidate for wiring your new 900 square ft addition? NO! If someone gets alot of top ratings for painting a room, does this make them the best company for removing the popcorn texture and smoothing up your ceilings? The answer is no! You cannot trust a person that does well in one area and is expected to deliver the same quality work when overhead expenses becomes a factor. 

The solution: We really want to build a product that scales, but the fact is… In order to provide consistent quality, we cannot build this system to effectively maintain high quality service providers without inspecting each job performed. What this means is we will personally inspect a certain amount of a service providers first jobs, this will allow us to keep them or cut them. Though this system may not be very scale able, we feel it will be the only way to truly ensure the highest quality of work.
